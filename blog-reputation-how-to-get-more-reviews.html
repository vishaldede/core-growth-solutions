How businesses get more Google reviews

By Core Growth Team • March 10, 2025 • 5 min read

Online reviews are the new word-of-mouth. For local businesses, a steady stream of positive Google reviews can mean the difference between thriving and just surviving. But getting customers to actually leave a review is often the hardest part.

In this guide, we’ll share proven, ethical strategies to increase your Google review count—and how AI Reputation Intelligence can help you monitor and respond at scale.

1. Make it ridiculously easy

The number one reason customers don’t leave reviews is friction. If they have to search for your Google Maps listing, click through multiple pages, and sign in, many will give up. Create a direct review link using your Google Place ID and share it everywhere:

  • In email signatures
  • On receipts and invoices
  • Via SMS after a purchase
  • As a QR code at your checkout counter

Tools like our AI platform can automate these touchpoints and track which channels generate the most reviews.

2. Timing is everything

Ask for a review when the customer is happiest. For a restaurant, that might be right after they compliment their meal. For a service business, immediately after a successful job. Use automated follow‑up emails or texts triggered by a purchase or service completion. The closer the ask to the positive experience, the higher the response rate.

3. Personalize your request

Generic “please leave a review” messages get ignored. Mention the customer’s name, what they purchased, and why their feedback matters. Example: “Hi Sarah, we’re so glad you enjoyed your plumbing repair. Could you share your experience on Google? It helps other homeowners find trusted help.”

4. Respond to every review (yes, even the negative ones)

When potential customers see that you actively engage with reviewers, they’re more likely to trust you and leave their own feedback. A thoughtful response to a negative review can even win back a customer. Our AI Reputation tool can suggest personalized responses based on sentiment analysis, saving you time while maintaining a human touch.

📈 Pro tip: Use AI to analyze review patterns

Instead of guessing why reviews are dropping, let AI do the work. AI Reputation Intelligence scans every review to detect emerging issues—like a sudden spike in complaints about wait times—so you can fix problems before they hurt your rating.

5. Offer an incentive (the right way)

Google’s guidelines prohibit offering rewards in exchange for reviews, but you can still encourage feedback by entering reviewers into a monthly drawing for a gift card (as long as it’s not contingent on a positive review). Be transparent: “Leave a review and be entered to win a $50 Amazon gift card—we appreciate all feedback!”

6. Feature reviews on your website and social media

When customers see their words showcased, they feel valued and are more likely to review you again in the future. Embed a Google Reviews widget on your homepage, or share screenshots of great reviews on Instagram. Tag the reviewer (with permission) to make it personal.

7. Train your team to ask

Your front-line staff are your best ambassadors. Teach them a simple script: “If you’re happy with our service today, would you mind leaving us a Google review? It really helps our small business.” A little training can turn every employee into a review generator.

8. Monitor and act on feedback

More reviews mean more data. Use that data to improve. If multiple customers mention slow service, it’s time to adjust staffing. If they rave about a particular product, feature it more prominently. Our AI dashboard tracks sentiment trends and alerts you to shifts in real time, so you can react fast.

Start getting more reviews today

You don’t need to beg for reviews—you need a system. Implement these tactics, and you’ll see your review count grow. And when you’re ready to take it to the next level, AI Reputation Intelligence can automate the entire process, from asking to analyzing.
