How to respond to bad Google reviews

By Core Growth Team•March 5, 2025•5 min read

No business is perfect. Eventually, you’ll get a negative review. How you respond can make or break your reputation—and even turn a detractor into a loyal customer.

1. Stay calm and don’t take it personally

Your first instinct might be to fire back, but resist. Take a deep breath and remember that your response is public. Future customers will judge you by how professionally you handle criticism.

2. Respond quickly

A fast reply shows you care. Aim to respond within 24–48 hours. If you use AI Reputation Intelligence, you can set up alerts for new reviews so you never miss one.

3. Apologize and empathize

Even if the customer is wrong, acknowledge their feelings. “I’m sorry to hear your experience wasn’t up to our standards” goes a long way. Avoid being defensive.

4. Take it offline

Provide a way to continue the conversation privately: “Please email us at support@yourbusiness.com so we can make this right.” This prevents a back‑and‑forth argument in public.

5. Highlight what you’ve learned

If the review points out a genuine issue, mention how you’re addressing it. “We’ve retrained our staff on this exact point—thank you for bringing it to our attention.”

🤖 AI can help you craft the perfect reply

6. Learn from every negative review

Track patterns. If multiple customers complain about the same thing, it’s a red flag. Use AI analytics to spot trends before they hurt your overall rating.

Turn critics into advocates

A well‑handled negative review can actually boost trust. Customers see that you’re responsive and committed to improvement. Start replying smarter today.
